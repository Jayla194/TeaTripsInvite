The brief

Before you dive in

🍵

What is Tea Trips?

You know how planning a trip means 10 tabs open — Booking.com, Google Maps, a Notes app, a spreadsheet you abandoned? Tea Trips fixes that. Search for spots, build an itinerary, see it all on a map, share it with friends (coming soon). It's genuinely a good idea. It just needs you to break it first.

⏱

How long does it take?

10 minutes minimum, as long as you want maximum. Even just poking around for a bit and leaving one sentence of feedback is more useful than nothing. I will not be checking how long you spent. I will not guilt you. (I will be refreshing the form responses obsessively, but that's my problem.)

🧪

What should I do?

Make an account, save some places, build a trip either manually or with the AI generator. Then try to break it. Book a trip in 1823. Search for something nonsensical. Do the thing you'd normally do and see if it handles it. If it doesn't, that's exactly what I need to know.

⚠️

One small note

Feel free to try unusual or extreme inputs (that's encouraged!), but when filling out the feedback form, please base your ratings on normal use - as if you were genuinely planning a trip.

If something only breaks under unrealistic conditions, feel free to report it as a bug, but don't worry about it affecting your overall rating.

Quick heads-up

Before you start testing

⚠️

Because this is a student project hosted on free services:

  • The site may take up to 30-60 seconds to load on first visit (server waking up)
  • If nothing loads or you see an error, the database may have gone to sleep after inactivity

This is expected behaviour and not a bug in the system itself.

If something still feels broken after waiting, definitely report it - that's exactly what I need to know.

Testing flow

The "official" testing flow

Think of this as a suggested route through the app. You don't have to follow it but if you're staring at the screen wondering what to do, start here.

Explore a region

Browse some tea spots. Open one that looks interesting. Does it make sense? Does it feel right? Would a normal person understand it?

Build a day trip

Add a couple of stops and check the itinerary view. Does the order seem logical or does it have you driving in circles?

Try to break it

Book a trip in the year 2157. Search for "asdfjkl". Add 40 stops to one day. Be chaotic. I have fixed some of this. I have not fixed all of it. Some of it may be things users would actually want.

Tell me everything

What was confusing, what was missing, what happened that you didn't expect. Good and bad — it all helps.
